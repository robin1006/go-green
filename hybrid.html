What is Hybrid Plants?
A hybrid is a cross between plant varieties
to get the values attributes of each variety.
Hybrids are developed for disease resistance, size, flowering,
colour, taste and any reason
a plant might be considered special.
Most modern plants currently on sale are hybrids.
There's nothing unusual about
creating hybrid plants.
It happens in the nature all the time,
when two different species cross polinate.
Commercial hybrid come about
after a deal of work.
Seeds for the popular commercial hybrids, like
'sungold' and 'Every Girl' tomatoes
have to be crossed, harvested and saved every year.
The greeder who first create a hybrid
owns the rights to it,
which is why they can be more expensive
than non-hybrids.
They guard the parentage of their hybrids closely.
